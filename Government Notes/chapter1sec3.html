Chapter 1 Section 3 - The Types of Government

  • Autocracy - The power and authority to rule are in the hands of a single individual
  • Monarchy - A king, queen, or emperor exercises the supreme powers of government
  • Oligarchy - Any system of gevernment in which a small group holds power
  • Democracy - Any system of gevernment in which rule is by the people
  • Republic - Voters are the source of the governments authority, elected representatives who are responsible to the people exercise that power

Major Types of Government

  • Autocracy
    • Totalitarian Dictatorship (Stalin,Hitler)
    • Absolute Monarchy
    • Constitutional Monarchy (Great Britain)
  • Oligarchy - small group (China)
  • Democracy - People Rule
    • Direct Democracy (People directly vote on issues)
    • Indirect Democracy/Republic (People have representatives that vote on their behalf) - USA government

Characteristics of a Democracy

  1. Free Elections
  2. Competing Political Parties
  3. Individual Liberty
  4. Majority rule with minority rights
  5. Civil Liberties

The Soil of Democracy

  1. Active Citizen Participation
  2. A favorable economy
  3. Widespread Education
  4. Strong Civil Society
  5. A Social Consensus

Extra Vocabulary

  • Political Party - A group of individuals with broad common interests who organize the nominate candidates for office, win elections, conduct government, and determine public policy
  • Free Enterprise - The opportunity to control one's economic decisions provides a base for making independent political parties
