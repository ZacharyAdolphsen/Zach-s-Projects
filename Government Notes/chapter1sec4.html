Chapter 1 Section 4 - Economic Theories

Key Terms

  • Economics - The study of human efforts to satisfy seemingly unlimited wants through the use of limited resources
  • Capitalism - Economic system in which freedom of choice and individual incentive fo rworkers, investors, consumers, and business enterprises is emphasized
  • Free Market - Buyers and sellers were free to make unlimites economic decisions in the marketplace
  • Laissez-Faire - "to let alone", the government should keep its hands off the economy. The governments role is limited to ensure free competition in the marketplace
  • Socialism - The government owns the basic means of production, determines the use of resources, distributes the products and wages and provides social services
  • Bourgeoisie - Owns the means of production and workers(Communism)
  • Proletariat - Work to produce the goods(Communism)
  • Communism - One class would evolve, property would all be held in common, and there would be no need for government
  • Command Economy - Decisions are made at the upper levels fo government and handed down to managers

Characteristics

    Capitalism
  • Private Ownership and control of property and economic resources
  • Free Enterprise
  • competition among businesses
  • Freedom of Choice
    Socialism
  • The distribution of wealth and economic opportunity equally among people
  • Society's control, through it government, of all major decisions about production
  • Public ownership of most land, of factories, and other means of production

The 3 Economic ?'s

  • What/how much should be produced?
  • How to produce?
  • For whom to produce for?

Economic Difference Between Socialism and Capitalism

    Capitalism
  • Marked Economy
  • Private Ownership
    Socialism
  • Planned/Command Economy
  • Public ownership of means of production

Political Difference Between Socialism and Capitalism

    Capitalism
  • 2 Party System
  • Individual Freedoms
  • Right to Criticize and oppose state
    Socialism
  • Single Party System
  • Repressive Government
  • Criticism and opposition is not tolerated

Democratic Socialism - Democratic political system with party socialist economic system (Scandanavian Countries)
