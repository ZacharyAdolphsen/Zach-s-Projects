What is a State?

A State is a political community that occupies a definite territory

4 Features of a State

  • Population - The people who live there
  • Territoy - Land with boundries
  • Sovereignty - Power to Rule
  • Government - Institution that includes public polocies written on behalf of the people

4 Theories of the origin of the State

  • Evolutionary - Family, clan, tribe, tied to the land
  • Force - Using power to manipulate
  • Divine Right - Be in the royal bloodline
  • Social Contract - Agreement to give up individual freedoms for the betterment of society

    • People related to Social Contract would be Thomas Hobbes and John Locke

Purposes of Government

  • Maintain Social Order (examples: Law Enforcement, Courts, and Laws)
  • Provide Public Services (examples: Schools, Social Welfare, CPS, and Water)
  • Provide National Security (examples: Military, Law Enforcement, National Guard, and Homeland Security)
  • Make Economic Decisions (examples: Taxes, Stimulus, FAFSA, and Interest Rates)
